# Token Labs Ltd — Company Formation Q&A

A record of decisions and answers used when filling out the 1st Formations incorporation form for Token Labs Ltd.

---

## Company Structure

### Q: Should the company be "limited by shares" or "limited by guarantee"?

**A: Limited by shares.**

A limited-by-shares structure is for for-profit companies where owners intend to take profits (dividends). Limited by guarantee is for non-profits and charities. Since Token Labs Ltd is a commercial SaaS product with the goal of generating revenue and eventual payouts, limited by shares is the correct choice.

## Business Activity & Classification

### Q: Which business activity category should be selected?

**A: Category J — Information and Communication.**

This category contains all software and IT development codes, including SIC codes 58xxx and 62xxx.

### Q: Which specific SIC code should be selected?

**A: 62012 — Business and domestic software development (primary), optionally with 58290 — Other software publishing as a secondary code.**

- **62012** covers the core product: building and developing AI-powered code-review software.
- **58290** covers the distribution/selling side (SaaS subscription model).

**Important:** Do not select 62011 (interactive leisure and entertainment software) — that's for games and entertainment products, not developer tools.

## Registration Tier & Services

### Q: Should the Privacy package or the All Inclusive package be chosen?

**A: Privacy package (~£121.59 inc VAT in year one).**

**Why Privacy, not All Inclusive:**
- **All Inclusive costs £39.99 + £100 = ~£140** and adds VAT Registration + PAYE Registration services.
- **VAT Registration** is explicitly out of scope for Token Labs Ltd (not required until £90k turnover per REVUE-381 AC).
- **PAYE Registration** is free to register directly with HMRC anytime you need payroll — no need to pre-buy it.
- The other All Inclusive extras (business address, GDPR templates, meeting room hours) are premature or irrelevant for a bootstrapped solo SaaS.

**Privacy is the right fit:** it includes the two core address-privacy services (Registered Office + Director Service Address) and nothing you don't need.

### Q: What is the expected renewal cost?

**A: Approximately £65/year ongoing (£39 for Registered Office + £26 for Director Service Address).**

The £121.59 is the **year-one cost only**. After that, budget ~£65/year to keep the address-privacy services active. If you let them lapse, your address exposure can return.

## Address Privacy

### Q: How does the address privacy work, and what addresses should be used where?

**A: Three addresses, three different visibility levels:**

| Address | Visibility | What to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Registered Office** | Public | 1st Formations' London WC2 address (included in Privacy package) |
| **Director Service Address** | Public | 1st Formations' London WC2 address (included in Privacy package) |
| **Usual Residential Address (URA)** | Private | Your actual home address — never published by Companies House |

**Key rule:** Your home address goes **only** in the Residential/URA field, which is private. The two public addresses (Registered Office + Service Address) are both handled by 1st Formations' service and appear on the public register — but they're not your home, so your home stays private.

## Nature of Control (PSC Declaration)

### Q: What should be selected for "Nature of Control" as a sole founder/shareholder?

**A: Select the following for yourself:**

| Field | Answer |
|---|---|
| Ownership of shares | **75% and above** (you own 100%) |
| Voting Rights | **75% and above** (you own 100% of votes) |
| Appoint or remove majority of board | **Yes** (you hold >50% of votes per Companies House guidance) |
| Other Significant influences or control | **No** |
| Controlling influence over a Firm(s) | **No** |
| Controlling influence over a trust(s) | **No** |

As the sole director/shareholder/PSC, you have absolute control across all dimensions — reflect that with the highest brackets and "Yes" where applicable.

## Share Holdings

### Q: How many shares should be issued, at what price?

**A: 100 ordinary shares at £1.00 GBP per share.**

- **Quantity: 100** — this is the accountant-friendly standard for a solo founder. It allows clean percentage splits later (e.g., 10 shares = 10% for a future co-founder) without restructuring. A single share at £1 is also valid but less flexible.
- **Price per share: £1.00** — the nominal value. This is your maximum unpaid liability on the shares; it's not money you pay now.
- **Class: ORDINARY** — standard voting shares with full rights.
- **Particulars:** Use the prescribed standard text (full rights to notice, attendance, voting at general meetings; one share = one vote; full rights to dividends and capital distributions).

All 100 shares go to you, making you the 100% owner and sole PSC.

## Authentication & Residential Address

### Q: Should the "Authentication Consent" checkbox be ticked?

**A: Yes — tick it.**

This confirms that you (as the sole subscriber/founder shareholder) consent to your name being used to electronically authenticate the memorandum of association. This is required for electronic incorporation and replaces a wet signature.

### Q: What if the "I have changed my name in the past 20 years, or moved home within the past 12 months" checkbox appears?

**A: Only tick it if it's actually true for you.**

- Moved home in the last 12 months → tick it (Companies House will ask for your former address for fraud checks).
- Changed name in the last 20 years → tick it (Companies House will ask for your former name).
- Neither applies → leave it unticked.

### Q: What address should be entered in the Residential Address field?

**A: Your actual, truthful home address.**

This is a legal requirement under UK anti-money-laundering regulations. The address you provide:
- **Must be accurate** — lying here is a legal breach.
- **Will not be published** on the public register (this is the private URA field).
- **Will only be shared** by Companies House with credit reference agencies and specified authorities (police, HMRC, etc.) — not the general public.

## Service Address Selection

### Q: How should the Director Service Address be configured?

**A: Select the included 1st Formations London WC2 service address, and set the forwarding address to your home address.**

- **Service Address:** Use the included London address from the Privacy package — this is the public address that appears on the Companies House register as your director correspondence address.
- **Forwarding Address:** Set this to your actual home address. This is where 1st Formations will forward your statutory mail (Companies House notices, HMRC correspondence, etc.). The forwarding address is private and not published.

This setup keeps your home address off the public register while ensuring you receive all official mail.

### Q: What about the "Business Address Service" (£96/year)?

**A: This is optional. Decline it or plan to cancel before the first renewal.**

- The **Business Address** is a separate service for general business correspondence on your letterhead/website. It's not required for company formation.
- It's bundled "free" in year one but renews at **£96/year**, which is unnecessary overhead for a CLI/SaaS product with no physical mail needs.
- **Decision:** If it's included free, leave it for year one but set a calendar reminder to cancel the renewal before it auto-bills.

## Personal Verification Code (PVC)

### Q: Should the PVC (11-character code) obtained from Companies House be entered?

**A: Yes — enter it.**

The PVC is your **Personal Verification Code** issued by Companies House after you completed identity verification (either via GOV.UK or another route). It's unique to you, not the company, and is reusable for any company you form.

Entering your existing PVC:
- Satisfies the mandatory ID-verification requirement upfront.
- Avoids post-incorporation verification chases.
- Is the same code — the terminology is interchangeable.

## Add-ons & Optional Services

### Q: Should the following add-ons be purchased?

#### 12-month Cancellation Protection (£14.99)
**A: No — decline it.**

You're forming a real company to operate, not planning to dissolve it. This service is irrelevant.

#### ICO Registration Service (£89.99)
**A: No — decline it and register directly with the ICO instead.**

- **Why:** The ICO registration is a legal requirement (any company processing personal data must register), but 1st Formations charges ~£90 as a markup.
- **Better option:** Register **directly with the ICO** (~£40–52/year) after you have your company number. It takes ~10 minutes online and costs roughly half the agent's fee.
- **Timeline:** You'll do this as part of REVUE-381 AC after incorporation completes.

#### Xero — 100% off for 6 months (Free)
**A: Decline or skip.**

- Xero is a legitimate accounting software, but adding it at checkout is an impulse, not a decision.
- It's a trial that will auto-charge after 6 months — avoid starting a trial clock on a pre-revenue company.
- Pick your accounting stack deliberately later, once you have revenue.

#### Nationwide Finance — loan offer (Free)
**A: Decline.**

This is a debt-finance lead broker. "Free" means it will share your details with third parties. A bootstrapped SaaS doesn't need a loan, and unsolicited finance offers create friction.

#### UENI — website setup (Free)
**A: Decline.**

You already have revue.sh. This service is irrelevant.

#### Partner Services (e.g., Starling bank referral)
**A: Optional — use it or apply directly to the bank yourself.**

If Starling (or any other bank) is included as a partner referral, you can either:
1. Complete the referral block — 1st Formations pre-warms the application.
2. Skip it and apply directly to Starling after incorporation — same end result, cleaner (no third party in the loop).

Either approach works. The referral just adds convenience; there's no cost to you either way.

## Timeline After Submission

1. **ID Verification emails** — Companies House and/or 1st Formations send instructions for you to verify identity (or confirm PVC if already verified).
2. **Compliance review** — 1st Formations' compliance team may do a manual check (routine, usually clears within 1 working day).
3. **Certificate of Incorporation** — Once approved, Companies House issues your company number and Certificate of Incorporation (typically within 24 hours to 1 working day).
4. **Next steps** — Open business bank account, set up Stripe KYC, register with ICO, update legal documents.
